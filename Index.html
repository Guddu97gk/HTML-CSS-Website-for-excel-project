Types of Pivot Chart in Excel.

1.Column Chart

Step 1: Enter Your Data Enter Your Data Open Excel and enter your data in a spreadsheet.
Step 2: Select Data Highlight the data you want to include in the chart.
Step 3:Insert Column Chart Go to the "Insert" tab in the Excel ribbon. Click on "Column Chart" in the Charts group. Choose the desired column chart type (e.g., clustered column chart).
Step 4:Customize Chart (Optional)
Right-click on different elements of the chart to access formatting options.
You can add data labels, change colors, adjust axis scales, and more.
Step 5: Title Your Chart Click on the chart title area and enter a title for your column chart (e.g., "Comparison of Values").

2.Bar Chart

Step 1: Enter Data Enter your data into the spreadsheet. For this example, let's say you have categories in column A and corresponding values in column B.
Step 2: Select Data Highlight the data you want to include in the bar chart.
In this case, select cells A1 to B5.
Step 3: Insert Bar Chart
Go to the "Insert" tab in the Excel ribbon.
Click on "Bar Chart" in the Charts group.
Choose the "Stacked Bar" or "Clustered Bar" chart type.

3.Line Chart

Step 1: Enter Data
Enter your data into the spreadsheet.
Step 2: Select Data
Highlight the data you want to include in the line chart. In this case, select cells A1 to B5.
Step 3: Insert Line Chart
Go to the "Insert" tab in the Excel ribbon.
Click on "Line Chart" in the Charts group.
Choose the "Line with markers" or "Line" chart type.
The line chart will be inserted into your worksheet.
You can further customize it as needed.

4.Pie-Chart

Step 1: Enter Data
Enter your data into the spreadsheet.
Step 2: Select Data
Highlight the data you want to include in the pie chart.
In this case, select cells A1 to B5.
Step 3: Insert Pie Chart
Go to the "Insert" tab in the Excel ribbon.
Click on "Pie Chart" in the Charts group.
Choose the "2-D Pie" or "3-D Pie" chart type.

5.Doughnut Chart

Step 1: Enter Your Data
Enter the data you want to represent in the Doughnut Chart.
Step 2: Select Your Data
Highlight the data you've entered.
Step 3: Insert Doughnut Chart
Go to the "Insert" tab on the Excel ribbon.
In the Charts group, select "Doughnut Chart" from the Chart options.
Step 4: Customize Your Doughnut Chart
After inserting the Doughnut Chart, you can customize it further:
Change Chart Title:
Click on the chart title and edit it to describe your data. Adjust Data Labels:
Click on the data labels on the chart to customize or remove them. Modify Colors:
Click on individual segments of the Doughnut Chart to select them.
Right-click and choose "Format Data Series."
In the Format Data Series pane, you can change the fill color, border color, etc.
Adjust Hole Size:
Click on the Doughnut Chart to select it.
Right-click and choose "Format Chart Area."
In the Format Chart Area pane, you can adjust the hole size using the "Doughnut Hole Size" option.
Explode Segments (Optional):
If you want to emphasize a particular segment, you can "explode" it.
Click on the chart to select it.
Click on the specific segment you want to explode.
Drag the segment slightly away from the center.
Step 5: Save and Share
Once you've customized your Doughnut Chart, save your Excel file. You can also copy the chart to use in other documents or presentations.
